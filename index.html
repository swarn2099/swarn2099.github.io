So what is the Goal of BEST 2016?

We believe that the goal of BEST is to create a robot that efficiently navigates through a "farm" and picks up produce.


Well, how do we achieve our goal? According to us Warriors, it is a clever combination of strategy and innovation. The thing to keep in mind about the competition this year is how important strategy is, especially how it will work with the design of our robot. Nothing too heavy because it will be too slow. Nothing to light, or else it wont be able to keep any produce.

Strategy may include knowing which produce to acquire first, based on scarcity and ease. Learn more about that in the Task section

Let's Farm

Tomatoes. Lettuce. Corn. Water Valve. Pigs. So what is the strategy for Colonel Mill?
The game field in a very small representation of a farm and its components. There can be no wastage of time or energy on a farm due to the vast size and workload. Keeping that in mind, we designed Colonel Mill's path and priority in the same way.
For the first couple of eetings, we solely discussed our strategy. Let's take a look at the point-value break down to the right.

Item Points Priority
Tomatoes 20 1
Lettuce 15 1
Seeds 10 2
Corn 20 3
Water Valve 100/50 4
Our strategy is simple.
We will first go for the tomatoes and lettuce because they are shared and a scarce resource. Colonel Mill will then deposit the tomatoes and lettue in their respective bins. Next, we will acquire the seeds. We will then go to the corn and while depositing the seeds, our robot will get the corn. Finally, we will depesoit the corn in our team bin and then turn the water valve.
Our strategy flows, withoutht the need for Colonel Mill to double back. Learn more about the components of the robot below.
To the right you see Colonel Mill

Our robot has two main parts:
The Lyson and the Kernel Friction

The Lyson uses a turbine like system to simply sweep the lettuce up much like a certain vacuum cleaner.

Kernel Friction uses a clever puller system that rotates two pvc pipes due to a motor. We decided to put friction tape on the pvc pipes so that when it turns against the corn, the corn gets pulled off and into a bin.

The Experience

BEST Robotics has taught our team many important skills, including teamwork, leadership, creativity, problem solving. We learned how to function together therefore bonding us and making us more like a family. Just like a family, we hit quirks throughout the season. We Warriors are smart students, going to a challenging high school. There are bound to be a difference of opinions with so many strong opinated and intlegent members. The important thing we learned is by communicating our ideas freely, we can combine and achieve more. Because of this collaboration of minds, we learned problem solving, testing everyone's ideas out and harvesting the best solution, improving it all along the way. We make sure everyone is heard so it is not only a few people who gain from this competition, every single person on our team does.
